Mona Lisa

Leonardo Da Vinci

About 1503-1506
Italian Renaissance
Oil on poplar table
Louvre Museum, Paris
77x53 cm

The painting is synonymous with artistic mastery and enigmatic beauty. The Mona Lisa is famous for its refined painting technique. It represents a woman with an enigmatic smile, probably Lisa Gherardini, wife of a Florentine merchant.
She is depicted in half length, slightly turned to the left,
with hands clasped in the lap. The background is a hilly landscape arid and far. Leonardo’s meticulous nuanced technique and masterful use of atmospheric perspective create a realistic quality and a sense of depth in the landscape.
The Mona Lisa is one of the most visited works of art
in the world and it represents a cultural icon.

Each dot on the map represents a publication of the work, offering an immersion into its journey through time and space.
