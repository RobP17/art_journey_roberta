La Vierge du chancelier Rolin

Jan van Eyck

1435
Netherlandish Renaissance
Oil on panel
Louvre Museum (not exhibited), Paris
66x62 cm

The painting depicts Chancellor Nicolas Rolin kneeling in prayer before the Virgin Mary and the Child Jesus.
The work is characterized by extreme precision in details
and an almost photographic realism. The technique used allowed the artist to obtain an extraordinary rendering of light, colors and textures. It is considered a masterpiece of Flemish painting for its beauty, refined technique and religious significance. The painting is an expression of the patron’s Marian devotion and piety, and reflects the complex religious and artistic culture of the 15th century in Europe.
