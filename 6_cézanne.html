Les Joueurs de cartes

Paul Cézanne

1890-1895
Post-Impressionism
Oil on canvas
Musée d'Orsay, Parigi
47,5x57 cm

The painting is part of a series of different versions of this theme, considered iconic works of post-impressionism.
The painting depicts a game scene in a tavern.
Players, with impenetrable faces and knotty hands,
are immersed in a silent concentration. The geometric composition and the dark palette emphasize the tense atmosphere. The work, lacking an explicit narrative meaning, has been interpreted as a representation of peasant life
or reflection on the intellectual challenge of the game.
