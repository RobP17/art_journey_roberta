Olympia

Édouard Manet

1863
Realism / Pre-Impressionism
Oil on canvas
Musée d'Orsay, Paris
130,5x190 cm

Olympia depicts a prostitute lying on a bed posing as the Venus de Milo. The woman lying on a messy bed, naked and looking directly at the viewer. His realistic depiction, devoid
of embellishment, and his bold pose contrasted with the typical idealized female depictions of the time.
The work, famous for its crude realism and its representation without idealization, sparked a great scandal at the time of its exposure. Today considered a revolutionary masterpiece,
it paved the way for Realism and Impressionism.
