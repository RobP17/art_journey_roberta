Guernica

Pablo Picasso

1 maggio-4 giugno 1937
Cubism
Oil on canvas
Museo Nacional Centro de Arte Reina Sofía, Madrid
349,3x776,6 cm

A monumental work by Pablo Picasso, it depicts the devastation and anguish caused by the bombing of the Basque city
of Guernica during the Spanish Civil War. The use of black
and white increases the drama and intensity of the scene, emphasizing the brutality and senselessness of the war.
The distorted figures and fragmented forms convey the chaos and despair of the moment. The absence of color underlines
the lack of hope. Guernica is not just a representation of
a specific event: it is a universal symbol of the horrors of war and the suffering it inflicts on innocent people.
